Information on Food Allergy

As parents, you only want what is best for your children, take a read below and learn more about Food Allergies and what you can do to manage them.


What is a Food Allergy?

Food Allergies is an abnormal response to specific kinds of foods caused by the immune system.

Most Common Food Allergens

In Children, the most common food allergens are peanuts, cowâ€™s milk (dairy), wheat, pinenut, soy and eggs. However, not limited to just these, where the Australian FDA also includes, allergens such as shellfish, sesame and lupin.

Food Allergies are different from food intolerances.

You might have heard of Food intolerances, such as Lactose intolerance, which means intolerant towards milk. The difference is that with food intolerance is how it occurs: this is where the body has a chemical reaction towards a specific food, compared to an immune response in food allergies.


Symptoms of Food Allergy

- Hives

- Swelling of face and mouth

- Vomiting

- Abdominal discomfort or pain

- Wheeze or Trouble Breathing

- Cough

- Swelling of Tongue

- Diarrhoea


Severe food allergy in children

For some children, they may experience respiratory difficulties, such as anaphylaxis. Most schools and childcare services across Australia are required to have an anaphylaxis management policy in place. However, it can be hard to control or manage food that your child may come across in school. Therefore, education of staff, students and parents are vital.



Treatment

The most efficient way is to avoid the foods that are causing the allergy reactions from the diet completely.

There are 2 main types of medication that can be used to relieve the symptoms of an allergic reaction to foods:

Used to treat mild to moderate allergic reactions.

Warning: Some antihistamines can cause drowsiness. Please seek your general practitioner's advice, and avoid prescribing for children under the age of 2 years old.
